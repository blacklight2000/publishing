Current Classification System

Issues Applicable to Both General and Specialized Libraries


  1. Librarians function as glorified book clerks.
  2. The current system is designed to enable librarians to physically retrieve books for users. Nothing more.
  3. Users have to know exactly what book they are looking for. If they don't, they are SOL.
  4. A subject matter specific book search that relies on a rigid, inflexible, broad classification system designed to physically store books - anyone sees the disconnect?
  5. Discovery using an inefficient, cryptic, pedantic search system is a process of discovering yourself.
  6. You shall be discovering how much frustration you can take, how much energy you want to waste and how much time you want sucked out of you.
  7. Book searches done by physical browsing is a pretty desperate alternative.
